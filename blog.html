Now we know ancient egyptian's faces

A DNA extracted from a ancients 2000 years old egyptian mummies is offering a intriguing glimpse at what three men could looks like when they hung out on the Fertile Crescent, the genetic sleuthing led to highly detailed 3D constructions of a trio from an ancient Nile community

To re-create the faces of the three egyptian men, the Parabon team turned to mummy DNA sequenced by researchers in Germany, at the University of Tubigen and the Max Planck Institute for the Science of Human History. They then extracted single nucleotide polymorphisms, which are makers in the genome that correspond to specific human characteristics such as eye and hair color and disease predisposition
